About FoodSecInfo
FoodSecInfo: Ensuring Food Security for School-Aged Children in Hamilton, Ontario via information advocacy
Welcome to FoodSecInfo, an initiative that seeks to tackle food insecurity among school-aged children in Hamilton, Ontario. Our goal is to improve the overall health and wellbeing of students by connecting them and their families with appropriate community resources and organizations specialized in addressing food access challenges.
Food insecurity is a complex issue that demands a thoughtful, comprehensive approach. Recognizing this need, we conducted extensive interviews and consultations with Tina Moffat, Steve Buist, and Nancy Phelan – experts who are passionate about combating food insecurity. These efforts have shaped our understanding of the problem and our focus on addressing it by partnering with local food security organizations.
Information Advocacy
FoodSecInfo's primary advocacy tool this website which hosts essential information about various food security organizations operating within Hamilton. We provide crucial details such as the name, specialization (e.g., emergency food supply, hot meals, cooking classes, school lunches), descriptions of services provided, and online links to these organizations.
Also on this site, we offer an interactive quiz designed to match users with relevant food security services based on sociodemographic information, needs, and preferences of the participants. See the "quiz" page.
To fine-tune our assistance further – while respecting cultural practices – we also provide options for families having Halal or Kosher dietary restrictions. Specialized resources are available for women and newcomer mothers, addressing unique situations and concerns.
An Ongoing Project
Currently, our website serves as a proof of concept – providing innovative solutions by collating resourceful organizations at your fingertips. However, we continuously strive to expand its scope by adding more organizations, covering broader jurisdictions of Hamilton, and continually refining the questionnaire to establish a stronger support network for those in need.
At FoodSecInfo, we pride ourselves on employing information advocacy as an effective means of reducing food insecurity among Hamilton's school-aged children. By collecting relevant information on existing services and sharing this knowledge in a centralized resource accessible by all who can benefit from it, we endeavor to improve food accessibility and eliminate hunger for students across our region.
We invite you to explore the FoodSecInfo website and learn more about the incredible partner organizations that share our commitment to eradicating food insecurity in our community. Together, we can ensure that Hamilton's youth have access to nutritious meals and education about healthy eating choices that lay the foundation for their success and well-being now – and into adulthood.
